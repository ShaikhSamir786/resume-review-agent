# HR / Talent Acquisition Resume Reviewer Skill

## Description
Expert resume reviewer specialized in **Human Resources, Talent Acquisition, Technical Recruiting, and People Operations** roles. Combines deep HR domain knowledge with modern recruitment best practices to optimize resumes for HR positions. Measured not just on "sounds professional" but on quantitative HR impact signals, tooling breadth, stakeholder management, and career trajectory evidence.

## Target Roles
- HR Business Partner (HRBP)
- Talent Acquisition Specialist / Recruiter
- Technical Recruiter
- Senior Talent Acquisition Manager / Head of Talent
- Recruiting Manager
- People Operations (People Ops)
- HR Generalist / HR Manager
- Campus Recruiter
- DEI & Inclusion Specialist
- Total Rewards / Compensation Analyst

## Core Competencies

### HR & Recruitment Expertise (40%)
- Talent lifecycle: sourcing → screening → interviewing → closing → onboarding
- Candidate pipeline building (passive vs active sourcing, Boolean search)
- ATS systems (Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, BambooHR, iCIMS, Taleo)
- Interviewing & assessment frameworks (STAR, competency-based, behavioral)
- Offer management, negotiation, closing rates
- Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI) strategy, sourcing diverse talent, bias mitigation
- HR analytics: Time-to-Hire, Time-to-Fill, Quality of Hire, Offer Acceptance Rate

### Achievement & Business Impact (30%)
- Pipeline metrics (time-to-fill reduction, open reqs filled, hiring volume)
- Quality signals (offer acceptance rate, 90-day retention)
- Cost impact (cost-per-hire reduction, RPO savings)
- Stakeholder management: hiring manager NPS, executive presentation

### Structure & Modern HR Presentation (20%)
- Strong professional summary with HR focus
- Skills section (Recruitment Tools, HRIS, Boolean Search, etc.)
- Clear career progression with measurable HR metrics
- Certifications (SHRM-CP, SHRM-SCP, AIRS, LinkedIn Talent, etc.)

### Business Acumen & HR Strategy (10%)
- HR as strategic partner, not just administrative function
- Workforce planning, headcount strategy, org design signals
- Business impact framing (reduced hiring cost, accelerated growth, reduced attrition)

### Step 6 — Green/Red Flags (HR/Talent Lens — Strict Audits)

#### Green Flags — Require Self Verification
- ATS fluency demonstrated
- Multi-channel sourcing and diversified pipeline strategy
- Sourcing blueprint documentation and candidate overseeing strategy
- Hiring manager NPS improvement
- Evidence of inclusive sourcing diversity program

#### Red Flags
- **Time-to-hire tracking missing** — no sourcing data → cannot run TA function
- **Candidate description only** — no DEI sourcing strategy even at high level
- **ATS unaware** — claims to know Recruiter, AI sourcing — source transformation changes
- **Cold-outreach/template bulk mailing** — no personalization evidence
- **No HRIS system workflow** — claims prior-work priority

### Step 7 — Board Miscellaneous Options
- If sourcing metrics missed → confirm sourcing competencies (tools pass-list: Boolean search, sourcing tools, candidate outreach)
- If sourcing confident → validate sourcing efficacy (response rate, black words evidence)
- If sourcing metrics active → assess minor risk to sourcing function
- If sourcing metrics absent → consider tool-pass-list only.

### Step 12 — Interview Questions (HR/Talent Lens — 8–12)

#### Technical HR
1. Walk me through how you run a full-cycle recruiting process for a senior engineering role. What stages do you use, who owns each, and how long is your average time-to-fill?
2. How do you source candidates beyond Job boards? Walk me through your Boolean search strategy and show me an example query you've built.
3. Walk me through how you qualify a candidate in a phone screen — what questions do you ask and how do you score them?

#### Stakeholder Management
4. A hiring manager insists on hiring someone you scored as a "no hire." How do you have that conversation?
5. How do you present a diversity sourcing report to the executive team? Walk me through how you frame the data, what metrics you highlight, and what call-to-actions you propose.
6. What do you do when a hiring manager won't agree on role priorities? How do you align them?

#### Metrics & Impact
7. What's your most impressive TA metric? Walk me through how you achieved it and how you'd sustain it.
8. How do you measure candidate experience and what have you done to improve it?
9. How do you handle a month when your pipeline dries up? Walk me through the sourcing plan you'd put in place.
10. How do you manage your calendar when 8 roles are open and 25 candidates are in the pipeline?

#### Behavioral
11. Tell me about a time a candidate rejected an offer. What did you learn and what did you change?
12. Describe a time you had to say no to a hiring manager who wanted to interview an unqualified candidate. How did you frame it and what happened next?

### Step 13 — Final Recommendations
1. **Lead with metrics** — TA is numbers-driven; % points and absolute headcounts > generic statements
2. **Show tool depth, not breadth** — Know one ATS deeply (sourcing, interview scheduling, offer → onboard flow), show usage context, not just a list of login names
3. **Show diversity sourcing evidence** — DEI initiatives should be named and measurable (tier-sourcing, inclusive sourcing funnel)
4. **Remove duty-based bullets** — "Responsible for recruiting" → "Owned end-to-end recruiting for [N] roles, cutting time-to-hill by [X]%"
5. **Add sourcing proof** — personal sourcing (Boolean search, direct outreach track record) differentiates real recruiters from coordinators
6. **Add a stakeholder NPS** — e.g., "Engineers gave hiring manager a NPS of +…" — demonstrates communicating with hired teams
