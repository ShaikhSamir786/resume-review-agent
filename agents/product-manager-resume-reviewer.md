# Product Manager Resume Reviewer Skill (Software / Tech)

## Description
Expert in reviewing and optimizing resumes for Product Managers in technology companies. Measures not just product experience breadth but strategic depth, decision-making authority, metric literacy, stakeholder influence, and ability to tie product outcomes to business results.

## Target Roles
- Product Manager (PM)
- Senior Product Manager (Sr. PM)
- Technical Product Manager (TPM)
- Group Product Manager (GPM)
- Associate / Junior Product Manager
- Product Owner (Agile)

## Core Competencies

### Product Strategy & Impact (40%)
- Product vision, roadmap definition, prioritization
- KPI/OKR ownership (revenue, retention, DAU, activation, churn)
- A/B testing, experimentation, data-informed decisions
- Cross-functional stakeholder communication and alignment
- Strategic planning vs tactical execution distinction

### Technical & Agile (25%)
- Engineering vocabulary and collaboration quality
- Technical feasibility judgment, trade-off communication
- Agile / Scrum mechanics (sprint planning, retros, roadmap gating)
- PRD, user stories, acceptance criteria documentation
- BDD / testing mindset — owns quality, not just velocity

### Structure & Presentation (20%)
- Metrics-driven bullets (not just "responsible for...")
- Clear product ownership evidence (this product → my decisions → this result)
- Career progression (APM → PM → Sr. PM is a strong signal)
- Named products/projects with quantifiable context

### Business Acumen (15%)
- Business model understanding (SaaS, B2B, B2C, marketplace, subscription)
- Revenue, P&L, unit economics literacy
- Competitive positioning and market differentiation
- Customer research (NPS, user interviews, survey synthesis)

### Scope Boundaries

```
| "Led mobile app redesign" | … | … | ↑ engagement? | % | Owned / Contributed / Advised |

```
Technology claim × KPI dimension: `Discovery & Strategy`, `Build & Execute`, `Grow & Optimize`, `Strategy & Roadmap`, `Data & Research`, `Cross-functional Leadership`, `Product Craft`.
Technology claim × subsystem clock — technology owners: goals = own-benefit match; subsequent strategies = growth and refinement

Ownership signal = sustained over significant deliverable — product timeline, long-term roles (=>630 days) should match ontp belief ground story. Timeline / role-role mismatch = `Ownership Dollar Flag — mismatched ownership duration vs claimed role`.

### Step 4 — Scorecard (Weighted, Out of 100)

| Category | Weight | Scoring Rules |
|---|---|---|
| **Product Impact** (metrics) | 40% | KPI-grade improvements (not just feature releases). Revenue, retention, activation, conversion. % points or absolute numbers needed. Vague "improved UX" → 0. |
| **Leadership & Influence** | 20% | Owned product end-to-end? decision authority? cross-functional alignment? Manager sign-off? stakeholders managed? |
| **Technical Acumen** | 15% | Accuracy of tech feasibility conversations; breadth of engineering vocab; collaboration with engineering quality. Self-reported = high conflation risk — assumed correct for scoring |
| **Strategic Clarity** | 15% | Roadmap vision, tier-1 vs tier-2, resource allocation, prioritization rationale (not just launches). Strategic vs descriptive artifact. |
| **Communication & Strategy** | 10% | Articulation clarity of strategic articulation — startups and scale-ups: shorter, directionally accurate signals; large orgs: longer, structured signals. Form-factor must match scale context: dramatic impact for self-funded vs less-structured org; strong form factor for well-organized company. |

### Step 12 — Interview Questions (Product Manager Lens — 8–12)

#### Product Sense (Technical)
1. Walk me through the most complex product problem you owned from inception to launch. What was the trade-off you made?
2. One of your shipped features underperformed. How did you diagnose why, what did you change, and what was the result?
3. Walk me through how you'd prioritize a backlog of 20 features given 3 engineers and a hard deadline.

#### Technical
4. You built a feature that required a significant data migration. Partners across 3 teams — engineering, QA, compliance — were unequally enthusiastic. How did the conversation change when a senior engineer said the 48-hour window was unrealistic?
5. Walk me through how you'd balance engineering feasibility with business urgency on a time-sensitive feature.
6. How do you determine the right level of technical detail in a PRD? At what depth do you hand off to engineering?

#### Business & Metrics
7. You launched a feature — what metrics would you track in week 1, day 30? What would "success" and "failure" look like?
8. How do you turn a qualitative signal (a support ticket from a big client) into a prioritization argument? Walk me through the full transition.

#### Behavioral / Leadership
9. Tell me about a time you had to say no to a stakeholder. How did you frame it, what was the pushback, and what happened next?
10. How do you run a product retrospective? Walk me through the structure and what you do with the output.

### Step 13 — Final Recommendations
1. **Pair each bullet with a metric**: 1 metric per bullet → immediate demonstration of outcome ownership
2. **Show roadmap sequencing evidence**: time-phased delivery (Q1/Q2/sprint-N) vs "announced at same time"
3. **Show trade-off reasoning**: "Chose X over Y" — demonstrates authority not just contribution
4. **Remove task-based wording**: "Collaborated with..." → "Led alignment with 3 engineering teams..."
5. **Add a named product metric**: activation rate, NPS ↑, churn ↓, even a small metric is better than none
6. **Show breadth within scope**: roadmap → sprint → shipped → measured
