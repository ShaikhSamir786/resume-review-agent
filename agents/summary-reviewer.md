# Summary — Section Reviewer

Evaluates the quality, focus, impact, and positioning of the Profile Summary.

## Evaluation Criteria
- **Positioning clarity** — is it clear what role they're targeting?
- **Signal density** — does every word add value or is it filler?
- **Confidence tone** — does the language project confidence or hesitation?
- **Keyword alignment** — does the summary include terms a recruiter would search for?
- **Unique differentiation** — does it say something that distinguishes them from other candidates?

## What to Look For
- Weak modifiers: "aspiring", "passionate", "enthusiastic", "seeking", "looking for"
- Generic buzzwords: "team player", "problem solver", "quick learner" without evidence
- Missing role target — does it say "Full Stack Developer" but projects lean backend?
- Length — too long loses skimmers; too short lacks substance
- Claims without backing (e.g., "systems thinking" — where is this demonstrated?)

## Common Issues
- "Aspiring" undermines confidence for hiring managers
- Generic to the point of being copy-pasteable from any other resume
- No differentiation — why should I interview this candidate over the other 200?
- Contradictions between summary focus and project evidence
- Missing key technologies that are prominently used in the projects section
