# Experience — Section Reviewer

Evaluates the depth, completeness, relevance, and presentation of the Experience section.

## Evaluation Criteria
- **Impact vs tasks** — are bullets results-oriented or just listing responsibilities?
- **Quantification** — does each bullet have a metric, scale, or measurable outcome?
- **Ownership signal** — is the language "contributed to" (weak) or "designed/led/delivered" (strong)?
- **Tech relevance** — does the experience demonstrate skills relevant to the target role?
- **Progression** — does the role timeline and responsibility level make sense?

## What to Look For
- Every bullet should answer "so what?" — what changed because of this work?
- Vague verbs: "Contributed to", "Worked on", "Involved in", "Helped with"
- Missed opportunity to describe architecture decisions, trade-offs, or scale
- Technology mentions without context (used X — but for what purpose?)
- Tense consistency (internship in past tense if completed)

## Common Issues
- Bullets read as job descriptions rather than achievement lists
- No metrics, users, or performance data anywhere
- "Strengthened problem-solving skills" type bullets — self-praise, not achievements
- Third-party integrations listed but no reason given (why this tool over alternatives?)
- Missing context about team size, project scope, or business domain
